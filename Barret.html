Barrett sniper rifle

The Barrett M82 is a "Special Application Scoped Rifle" (SASR) developed and produced by the American Barrett Firearms Company. The gun and its derivatives Most of the armies of Western countries, including the US military. The US military nicknamed it "Light Fifty" because it uses the same 12.7×99 mm NATO (.50 BMG, 12.7 mm) large-caliber ammunition as the Browning M2 heavy machine gun. The gun has many derivatives, including the original M82, M82A1, M82A3 and the M82A2, which uses a bullpup design for easy portability. The subsequent XM500 also inherited its design spirit and used a bullpup design. The improved version adopted by the US military is named "M107" (the top of the upper receiver is equipped with a full-size Picatinny rail), and the latest model currently launched by Barrett is a further improved version of the M107, M107A1.

M82A3 The 12.7×99mm Barrett M82A3 semi-automatic sniper rifle is a new product designed according to the M82A1M specifications. It has an extended tactical rail system and is a bit taller than the M82A1M/M107. However, unlike the M82A1M/M107, it does not have a rear slide rail and a fixed shaft socket. XM107/M107 It was originally the official name of the 12.7×99mm Barrett M95 bolt-action sniper rifle by the US military. It was later changed to the M82A1M improved version, with an extended tactical rail system, rear grip and fixed shaft socket.
